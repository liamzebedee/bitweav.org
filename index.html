What is BitWeav?

BitWeav offers threaded microblogging (like Twitter) and encrypted private text, audio and video chat (like Skype), but with three significant advantages to its design:

  1. Decentralised and P2P — the BitWeav microblogging network is constructed entirely from those who use it. Instead of relying on another company's servers to store content, an approach vulnerable to censorship, comprimise of security and loss of privacy, each user controls their own profile. The BitWeav software socialises with other users automatically to distribute content that they are both intereted in.
  2. Security — every message you send and receive from other people is guaranteed to be entirely secure, meaning that you know it has full integrity and is completely authentic. Private communications are also encrypted from other third parties eavesdropping (like the NSA).
  3. Reliability — the usability and security of BitWeav is not for comprimise, a principle for present and future. BitWeav is a free and open-source project, meaning that you are entitled to the product for free and have access to the source code so that you may modify, study, improve and share your modifications (so everyone may benefit).

For hackers.
